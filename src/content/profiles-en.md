Bian Shirui is a member of the Communist Party of China. He earned his B.S. in Mathematics and Applied Mathematics from the School of Mathematical Sciences at Fudan University in 2023 (Class of 2019) and is currently a direct Ph.D. student in Applied Mathematics at Fudan University (Class of 2023). He is also a member of the inaugural "Mathematical Talent Experimental Class" at Fudan University. His advisor is Professor Wei Lin. His research interests include potential energy modeling of stochastic oscillation dynamics, critical transition warning, and intelligent control in complex living systems. He is funded by the inaugural National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC) Youth Student Basic Research Project (PhD Student) and was selected for the inaugural China Association for Science and Technology (CAST) Youth Talent Cultivation Program for PhD Students. He has published four first-author academic papers in journals such as *Advanced Science*, *Cell Reports Physical Science*, and *Chaos*. He currently serves as a Young Associated Editor for the AIMS journal *Discrete and Continuous Dynamical Systems - Series I: Intelligence*. He received the Undergraduate Paper Award (The UPA) at the 2025 International Congress of Chinese Mathematicians (ICCM 2025) and the First Prize in the "Questions and Conjectures" activity of the 3rd Top-notch Innovative Talents Cultivation Program 2.0 by the Ministry of Education, among other awards. He was awarded the National Scholarship for PhD Students in 2024 and 2025, and was named Shanghai Outstanding Graduate in 2023. He is the inaugural Executive Chairman of the Fudan University Student Association for Science and Technology. He has been named Fudan University Outstanding Student and Outstanding League Member for five consecutive years, and was honored as Fudan University Outstanding Student Pacesetter in 2024. He serves as President of the Sports Club of the School of Mathematical Sciences, Captain of the Football Team, and Captain of the Badminton Team, having won more than 30 awards in university- and college-level sports competitions.
